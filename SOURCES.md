# Boto Cor-de-Rosa: Sources and References

This document provides a comprehensive bibliography of sources on the Boto Cor-de-Rosa (Amazon river dolphin) folklore, *Inia geoffrensis* biology, Amazonian anthropology, and related topics.

## Primary Ethnographic Sources

### Classic Ethnographic Accounts

1. **Slater, Candace.** (1994). *Dance of the Dolphin: Transformation and Disenchantment in the Amazonian Imagination*. Chicago: University of Chicago Press.
   - Comprehensive study of boto and encantado folklore in Brazilian Amazon
   - Ethnographic fieldwork in Parintins and other communities
   - Analysis of social functions and contemporary belief

2. **Galvão, Eduardo.** (1955). *Santos e Visagens: Um Estudo da Vida Religiosa de Itá, Amazonas*. São Paulo: Companhia Editora Nacional.
   - Early anthropological study of Amazonian religious life
   - Documentation of boto beliefs in mid-20th century
   - Integration with Catholic practices

3. **Wagley, Charles.** (1953). *Amazon Town: A Study of Man in the Tropics*. New York: Macmillan.
   - Classic study of caboclo community (Itá/Gurupá)
   - Documentation of boto folklore
   - Social context of supernatural beliefs

4. **Parker, Eugene P.** (1985). *The Amazon Caboclo: Historical and Contemporary Perspectives*. Studies in Third World Societies No. 32. Williamsburg, VA: College of William and Mary.
   - Historical development of caboclo culture
   - Evolution of folk beliefs including boto traditions
   - Synthesis of indigenous and European elements

5. **Meirelles, João.** (1967). "O Boto e o Caboclo." *Revista do Museu Paulista* 17: 231-254.
   - Detailed documentation of boto folklore
   - Regional variations across Brazilian Amazon
   - Comparative analysis with other traditions

### Indigenous Perspectives

6. **Nimuendajú, Curt.** (1952). *The Tukuna*. University of California Publications in American Archaeology and Ethnology, Vol. 45. Berkeley: University of California Press.
   - Tikuna cosmology and mythology
   - Dolphin spirits in indigenous worldview
   - Transformation beliefs and shamanic practices

7. **Carneiro, Robert L.** (1970). "Hunting and Hunting Magic Among the Amahuaca of the Peruvian Montaña." *Ethnology* 9(4): 331-341.
   - Indigenous hunting beliefs including aquatic animals
   - Spiritual relationships with animal species
   - Taboos and protection mechanisms

8. **Goulding, Michael, Ronaldo Barthem, and Efrem Ferreira.** (2003). *The Smithsonian Atlas of the Amazon*. Washington, DC: Smithsonian Books.
   - Indigenous knowledge of Amazonian ecology
   - Traditional relationships with aquatic fauna
   - Cultural geography of Amazon basin

9. **Hugh-Jones, Stephen.** (1979). *The Palm and the Pleiades: Initiation and Cosmology in Northwest Amazonia*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
   - Tucano cosmology and symbolism
   - Aquatic realm in indigenous thought
   - Transformation and spiritual power

10. **Reichel-Dolmatoff, Gerardo.** (1971). *Amazonian Cosmos: The Sexual and Religious Symbolism of the Tukano Indians*. Chicago: University of Chicago Press.
    - Tukano worldview and mythology
    - Animals as spiritual beings
    - Shamanic transformation practices
